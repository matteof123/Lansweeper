## Company Overview

Lansweeper is a Cyber Asset Intelligence and IT Asset Management (ITAM) platform founded in 2004, headquartered in Merelbeke, Belgium. The company provides agentless discovery and inventory of IT, OT, and IoT assets across hybrid environments. With ~400 employees, $40M annual revenue, and $158M in funding (Insight Partners + Dovesco), Lansweeper monitors 100M+ connected devices for 25,000+ customers worldwide including Mercedes, FC Barcelona, IBM, Hitachi Energy, and Rentokil. Owned by Dovesco since 2018, with sub-organizations RedJack (passive network discovery, acquired 2025) and RankedRight (vulnerability prioritization).

## Offer & UVP

Lansweeper sells a unified asset intelligence platform that discovers, inventories, and manages all technology assets — IT, OT, and IoT — from a single pane of glass.

**Core Differentiators:**
1. **Agentless discovery across IT + OT + IoT** — No agents needed. Scans networks passively and actively to find every connected device, including PLCs, SCADA, sensors, and unmanaged/rogue assets. Integrates OT protocols from Siemens, Rockwell Automation, Mitsubishi Electric, and Schneider Electric.
2. **150,000+ assets scanned, reports in 15-20 seconds** — Enterprise-grade performance at scale (Hitachi Energy case study). Single pane of glass across global estates.
3. **Compliance & audit readiness out of the box** — Automated tracking for SOX, PCI-DSS, Cyber Essentials Plus, CIS. Weekly scanning discipline and audit-ready logs of all IT assets and history.
4. **RedJack passive network discovery** — 2025 acquisition brings advanced passive discovery and application dependency mapping for unknown/unmanaged assets without touching production systems.
5. **Cloud + on-prem flexibility** — Full cloud platform with on-prem option for regulated industries (banking, government, defense). AI features available in both deployment models.

## ICP

**Firmographic:**
- Industry: Manufacturing, Financial Services, Education (Higher Ed + K-12), Healthcare, Government/Public Sector, MSPs, Telecommunications, Utilities, Critical Infrastructure
- Company size: 1,000+ employees (enterprise focus for direct sales); MSPs managing 2K–100K assets per client
- Geography: Global — US, UK, EU (strong in Benelux), APAC
- Tech stack signals: ServiceNow, Qualys, Tanium, ManageEngine, SolarWinds (competitors or complementary tools); Active Directory; VMware/Hyper-V; Azure/AWS
- Funding stage: Established mid-market to enterprise; not startup-focused

**Personas:**

- **IT Director / VP of IT** — Enterprise IT leadership, 1000+ employee companies
  - Day-to-day: Managing IT infrastructure, overseeing asset lifecycle, reporting to CISO/CIO on compliance posture
  - Metrics they own: Uptime, patch compliance %, asset utilization, IT cost per employee
  - Biggest frustrations: Incomplete asset inventories, spreadsheet-based tracking, blind spots in the network, failed audits
  - Buying role: Decision maker
  - Target when: Hiring for IT infrastructure roles, recently promoted, company undergoing digital transformation or M&A
  - Skip when: Company under 500 employees, already a Lansweeper customer

- **CIO / CISO** — C-level technology/security leadership
  - Day-to-day: Board-level reporting on cyber risk, compliance strategy, vendor consolidation
  - Metrics they own: Risk score, compliance audit pass rate, security incident response time, TCO
  - Biggest frustrations: Cannot answer "what's on our network?" confidently, regulatory pressure, shadow IT
  - Buying role: Executive sponsor / final approver
  - Target when: Post-breach, upcoming audit, regulatory change, new compliance mandate
  - Skip when: Fully committed to ServiceNow ecosystem with no discovery gap

- **MSP Owner / Technical Director** — Managed Service Providers
  - Day-to-day: Managing multiple client environments, service delivery, client onboarding
  - Metrics they own: Client satisfaction, ticket resolution time, margin per client, compliance SLAs
  - Biggest frustrations: No unified view across client environments, manual asset discovery per site, can't scale onboarding
  - Buying role: Decision maker (often sole)
  - Target when: Growing client base, adding security services, losing visibility at scale
  - Skip when: Fewer than 10 managed clients, already using deep RMM with built-in discovery

## 2. Company Overview & UVP

### What Lansweeper Does
Agentless discovery and inventory of every technology asset — IT, OT, and IoT — across hybrid environments. One platform replaces fragmented spreadsheets, manual audits, and siloed tools with a trusted system of record.

### Why They Win
| Differentiator | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Agentless IT + OT + IoT discovery | Only platform scanning Siemens, Rockwell, Mitsubishi, Schneider Electric OT protocols alongside traditional IT |
| Enterprise scale | Hitachi Energy: 150,000 assets, reports in 15-20 seconds |
| Compliance automation | University of Derby: Cyber Essentials Plus via weekly scans; B2B Cyber Secure: audit-ready in minutes across government ministries |
| Cloud + on-prem flexibility | Banque BEMO reply confirms demand; regulated industries need on-prem with AI |
| Passive discovery (RedJack) | 2025 acquisition adds network traffic analysis for unknown/unmanaged assets |

### Competitive Landscape
| Competitor | Lansweeper Advantage |
|---|---|
| ServiceNow ITAM | Lansweeper deploys faster, costs less, and discovers OT natively. ServiceNow requires modules + professional services. |
| Qualys VMDR | Qualys is vulnerability-first; Lansweeper is asset-first with broader discovery (OT, IoT, unmanaged). |
| Tanium | Tanium requires agents; Lansweeper is agentless. Tanium is enterprise-only pricing. |
| ManageEngine AssetExplorer | Lansweeper has stronger OT discovery and larger enterprise case studies. |
| SolarWinds | Lansweeper has modern cloud platform; SolarWinds carries breach reputation risk. |

## 5. ICP & Persona Deep Dives

### Primary Persona: IT Director / VP of IT
- **Where they live**: LinkedIn, IT conferences (SITS UK, RSA Conference), Gartner reports
- **What triggers outreach**: Hiring for IT infrastructure roles, upcoming audit, M&A, device refresh
- **What resonates**: Concrete numbers ("found 130 unpatched servers in 24 hours"), peer proof (same-industry case studies)
- **What turns them off**: Generic pitches, vague claims, aggressive CTAs
- **Best email step**: Email 1 with metric-driven hook; Email 3 with case study

### Secondary Persona: CIO / CISO
- **Where they live**: Board meetings, Gartner, RSA, executive briefings
- **What triggers outreach**: Post-breach, regulatory change, vendor consolidation mandate
- **What resonates**: Risk reduction narrative, compliance automation, cost savings
- **Best approach**: LinkedIn first (via Heyreach), then warm email follow-up

### Tertiary Persona: MSP Owner / Technical Director
- **Where they live**: MSP-specific communities, vendor events, partner programs
- **What triggers outreach**: Client growth, adding security services, tool consolidation
- **What resonates**: "Scale without adding headcount," multi-site management, PSA/RMM integration

## 7. Campaign Performance Summary

As of 2026-03-30, campaigns run on SmartLead + Clay (not yet in EmailBison).

**Key metrics from calls:**
- ~0.7% positive reply rate on main IT campaigns
- Close-Lost campaign generating strongest engagement
- Website visitor campaigns driving pricing inquiries
- LinkedIn (Heyreach) producing event-related responses

**Positive reply patterns:**
- IT Directors and CIOs respond most positively
- Close-lost contacts convert at higher rate than cold
- OT-related messaging generates strong interest from industrial security roles
- Financial services contacts ask about on-prem + AI deployment
- Education contacts are budget-cycle-sensitive

**Negative reply patterns:**
- K-12 budget constraints (time sends for July fiscal year)
- Some contacts redirect to "the right person" (job title targeting can be tighter)
- Existing customers accidentally contacted (tighten exclusion lists)
